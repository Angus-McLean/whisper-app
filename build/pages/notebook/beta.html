Design Evolution

After the final in-class presentation where we got to test the system out with everyone, as well as several usability testings we’ve conducted, we decided to adopt the following design changes:

  1. Remove the seating plan and allow users to change their team on the main window instead

Instead of implementing a seating plan screen prior to joining the meeting (where users can only whisper to the group they’ve chosen), we’ve decided to allow users to choose their team in the main window:

Before                                                                                After

        There are several reasons behind this decision:

  1. Provide flexibility to users

Some users may not know which group to join prior to joining the meeting, and some might want to change their group and whisper to different people during the video conference. They might also want to be in a group of more than 4, so instead of complicating the design by introducing flexible seating plans (which will in turn increase the number of decisions users need to make upfront), we’ve decided to provide such flexibility in the main window where users can make changes to their team anytime during the meeting. This also goes hand in hand with the 3rd Usability Heuristic by Jakob Nielsen, User control and freedom.

  1. More consistent with industry standard video conferencing tool

Whispering is an add on function to video conferencing but not the main function (which is to listen and chat with people remotely), so users should not be required to make decisions on whether they want to whisper, and who they want to whisper with, before joining the meeting. Therefore, by allowing users to go straight into the meeting room as soon as possible will result in a more natural flow, and they can then decide if they want to engage in whispers, and if so, with whom. Since most video conferencing tools do not require you to choose some sort of seating before joining the meeting, such a design change will make our system feel more familiar with the user and hence reduce confusion. This goes with the 4th Usability Heuristic by Jakob Nielsen, Consistency and standards.

  1. Easier to implement

A seating plan, especially a seating plan that allows flexibility, requires a lot more effort to implement compared to the switching of teams on the main window. Although this reason is not directly related to usability, we believe that this is still a valid reason because it will allow us to launch our product earlier into the market and test it out with real users. By doing so, we can get real data sooner and iterate on it quicker.

  1. Click on ‘everyone’ to initiate whisper to your group

In our alpha system, in order to whisper to everyone on your team you’d have to not select anyone on the list and then click ‘whisper call’. However, this has proven to be a bit confusing because it is easy to forget if you’ve chosen someone or not for whispering. To improve on it, we’ve decided to create an ‘everyone’ tab where you’d have to click on it if you want to initiate any sort of whispers to everyone on your team. This way, the action needed to initiate both ‘team’ and ‘private’ whispers is consistent, and because users will need to select something before engaging in whisper, it will also help reduce error such as whispering to the wrong person. This is consistent with the 5th Usability Heuristic by Jakob Nielsen, Error prevention, and the 4th Usability Heuristic, Consistency and standards.

  1. Greater distinction between whisper call and whisper voice messages button

In our alpha prototype we changed the icons in light of the feedback that we’ve received in the formative feedback about them being ambiguous. In the beta prototype, we have modified it even further by adding colours that match the general ‘call icon’ being green, and the ‘record’ icon being red. This modification goes with the 4th Usability Heuristic by Jakob Nielsen, Consistency and standards, since it corresponds to industry standard designs.
